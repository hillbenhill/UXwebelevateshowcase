                    <h2><u>Mary's' Scenario</u></h2> 
<p>Mary the community nurse – part of her regular rounds it looking after some of the residents living in sheltered 
accommodation.<br> When on duty she logs into the patient monitoring dashboard and is updated with the health screening 
charts of her patients. One of the elderly residents has diabetes, hypertension and arrhythmia.<br>
Upon login she is able pull up the patient's chart for the past week.<br> If consistent readings are outside of normal, 
an alert is generated and flagged with the current logged in user. <br>Mary gets an alert and can see that the patient in 
question is showing very high blood pressure and poor control of blood glucose over the last 48-72 hours. 
She moves the case review into her high priority todo list for her daily rounds.<br>  
Upon seeing the patient she is able to take a blood pressure reading and review the level of compliance with the 
diabetes intervention regime.<br>  Mary updates the patient’s data on the system and marks the task off as done.<br> 
With the data and the physical review Mary is able to make a judgment.<br>  Follow up with a doctor’s visit to investigate
 further or call in the emergency services.<br> The Gp will be sent an alert as part of the community team, assigned a follow 
 up call and view supporting data on the system.<br>
The system enables the community nursing team prioritise patient care based on a dynamic personalised need basis. <br></p>
