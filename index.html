1 What The Product Is

Product Name - Haven

Haven Concept Overview

With the rapid population ageing that is occurring world-wide, there is increasing interest in “smart home” technologies that can assist older adults to continue living at home with safety and independence.

Improved health and social care over recent years has increased life expectancy worldwide. As a result, in 2010 an estimated 524 million people were aged 65 or older - 8% of the world’s. By 2050, this number is expected to nearly triple to about 1.5 billion, representing 16% of the world’s population.

The current trends in population demographics make it increasingly difficult for governments worldwide to fully support the health and social care systems. Therefore, a large proportion of older adults rely solely on family members for assisted care, often resulting in great emotional stress for family caregivers.

As Diane Cook, lead researcher of Washington State University’s “Smart Home Projects” describes, “Individuals don’t want to leave their home even if they live in a rural area and don’t have easy access to healthcare and family members. They still will not leave their family home. Studies show that when they do – particularly against their will – that move is often followed by cognitive and physical decline and death”.

Smart-homes are purpose designed living spaces that provide interactive technologies and unobtrusive support systems to enable people to enjoy a higher level of independence, activity, participation or well-being than would otherwise be available. With these home based technologies there is potential for a person to live in their own home without the need to be hospitalised or institutionalised. Empowering the elderly to live independently instead of in an adult care facility not only provides financial savings, it also has the potential to improve quality of life, reduce the strain on aged care facilities, and provide relief for family caregivers.

However, we need a more viable and scalable solution to meet this demand. Enter a new wave of smart home technology known as “assistive domotics”.

Assistive domotics is an application of home automation that focuses on enabling elderly or disabled people to live at their home instead of a nursing home or similar healthcare facility. These products use sensors (eg: Bluetooth) to track movements in the home, or lack thereof, of the relevant resident. Sensors can be placed on any item, including doors, keychains, and even pillboxes. GPS technology documents precise location information of the person, logs activity, and reports it to a family member living somewhere else. Haven aims to harness all these available features and more. By using assistive domotics, some of the principal benefits Haven could provide to help independent living for the elderly are:

  • Emergency Response
  • Eyesight and hearing
  • Memory
  • Video/Audio monitoring
  • Medication Assistance

In addition to these immediate benefits to the resident, this type of technology provides support to those in the “sandwich generation,” a term coined for those simultaneously supporting aging parents and their children.

Product Definition

Haven is aimed at family, carers and medical professionals of the elderly or disabled people who wish to live in their home safely and independently for as long as possible.
By utilising assistive domotics such as wearable tech and sensors, Haven will record an individual’s body readings and track their activities inside and outside the house.
The carers will be able to unobtrusively follow a person’s daily routine by reading simple time line charts accessible via website or downloadable applications for tablets and smartphones.
Currently we believe there is a lack of appropriate applications for tablets, smartphones and systems which avail of smart home technology to monitor and care for the vulnerable who wish to remain in their home.

Assistive Domotics: A form of home automation which focuses on making it possible for older adults and people with disabilities to remain at home, safe and comfortable.
Futher development is needed in this area to help address the currrent burden on under resourced health services and gowth in ageing populations.
